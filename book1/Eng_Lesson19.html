LESSON-19

(Nineteenth Lesson)

The Characters in the Essential English book (3)

      The students

Here are Mr. Priestley's students. Look at them. They are just coming to his house for their lesson.
      You will often meet them in this book and the other books of Essential English. We are going to be present at their lessons and we are going to listen to them talking together.
      They are going to talk about their works and their holidays their countries and their lives their joys and their sorrows. They will tell you what they like and what they dislike. They will tell you stories. They will write letters, tell jokes, and sing songs for you. I hope that you will soon know them and like them, and feel that you and they are friends. Here is the first one to come.


This is Jan.
He is generally early.
He doesn't like to be a minute late for his lessons.
He is not only very clever, but he is also a very hard worker, and likes to do everything well.
He has rather dark brown hair and good grey eyes.
He is not very tall, but he is strong, swims well, and is a good footballer.

      And who is this?
This is Lucille. Lucille is beautiful and rich and gay.
She is tall and slim, with big dark eyes and black hair.
She goes out to dinner or dance or a theatre, almost every evening, and doesn't generally get to bed until very late at night or rather early in the morning, so you see Lucille doesn't do very much work.

This is Olaf.
isn't he a big fellow?
He is six foot three tall, he can walk and climb all day and he never feels tired.
He is as strong as a horse.
He has blue eyes and yellow hair.
He doesn't talk much, but he thinks a lot.

Here is Pedro.
He is very handsome, tall, and dark and well dress.
He is rich and clever, too.
He does everything well.
He is spanish, but he speaks english and french very well, he goes everywhere and can talk well about almost everthing music, pictures, other contries, literature and life.

      The next one to come in is Frieda.
She has brown hair and big grey eyes.
She doesn't talk much; she is rather quiet but is very kind and nice; they all like her.
I think that she is pretty and so do all the other students except Jan; he thinks that she is beautiful.

      And there is the last one. He is generally the last one to come, but the first to go away. This is hob He is certainly not handsome or hard working or clever. But he thinks that he is all these. He doesn't like swimming or football. He thinks that they are too much like hard work. He doesn't like study. He says that study makes him tired. He isn't polite, and he isn't well dress. He doesn't always listen to lesson in the class. (he sometime goes to sleep there). He isn't quiet; he talks a lot. He is a good eater and a good sleeper. But he is good-hearted and loves a joke. He knows a lot of funny stories and he always wants to tell them to you.
      Now they are going into Mr priestley's study. We will follow them in and meet them there in lesson 21.

GRAMMAR


Plural

In the sentence
      They are going to talk about their contries and their lives
You have in lives another plural form, a little different from the regular ones. lives [laiz] is the plural of life a number of noun that ending in f or fe change the f to ves to form the plural e.g

STUDY WORDS

Negative Prefix

We sometimes make a word negative by using prefix, e.g. un-, in-, im-, dis-
Here are negative of words in lesson 1 to 19


Negative
happy unhappy
healthy unhealthy
common uncommon
pleasant unpleasant
practical unpractical
Negative
like(adj) unlike
like(verb) dislike
correct incorrect
polite impolite
dress(verb) undress

Related Word

life [laif] ⤍ live [liv] ⤍ alive [əˈlaɪv]

I will tell you the story of my life
I live in london.
These rabbits are alive those are dead.


joy ⤍ enjoy ⤍ enjoyment

- They will tell you their joys and sorrows.
- I enjoy good music it gives great enjoyment.



sorry ⤍ sorrow ⤍ sorrowful

-He was very sorry that my friend was dead.
-He looked very sorrowful; there was sorrow in his face.



certain ⤍ certainly ⤍ uncertain

- Are you certain that you understand the work?
- I am uncertain about one or two things
- Hop is certainly not handsome or polite.



polite ⤍ politely ⤍ politeness ⤍ impolite

- He is not polite.
- He doesn't speak politely.
- He hardly know the meaning of politeness.
- he is very impolite.

fun ⤍ funny

- He will tell funny stories.
- She loves fun and gaiety.

study /ˈstʌd.i/ ⤍ to study ⤍ student /ˈstjuː.dənt/

- Hop doesn't like study.
- Mr Priestley is in his study.
- We are going to study english.
- The students are in Mr priestley's study.


going to

In lesson 19 you can see another way of expressing the future by using going to e.g.

       - We are going to be present at their lesson.
       - We are going to listen to them talking together.
       - They are going to talking about their holidays
