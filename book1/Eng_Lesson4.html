GRAMMAR

boy, woman, this, what, a, are, they, etc., are words
These are people: boy, girl, man, woman, teacher, king, queen, etc.
These are thing: table, chair, clock, car, mountain, book, pen, etc.
​
The word Who is for people.
The word What is for things.
But Note: Who is he? He is Mr. Brown. What is he? He is a teacher

The word for males (boy, man, husband, son, king etc.,) are Masculine Gender.

The word for females (girl, woman, wife, daughter, queen etc.,) are Feminine Gender.

The word for things (table, chair, clock, mountain, pen etc.,) are Neuter Gender.

"He", is for Masculine Gender (Singular).
    e.g. He is a boy., He is a son He is a king., He is a husband.

"She", is for Feminine Gender (Singular).
    e.g. She is a girl., she is a daughter., She is a queen., She is a wife.

"It", is for Neuter Gender (Singular).
    e.g. It is a train., It is a picture it is an egg., it is a car.

"They", is for masculine, feminine and Neuter Gender (Plural).
    e.g. They are boys., They are girls., They are eggs.
