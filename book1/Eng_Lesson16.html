Subjects and Object


There are two forms for some of the pronouns, one when the pronoun is the subject of the verb, another when it is the object of the verb.
here are some very simple sentences. in the first column you​​ have the subject of the verb. in the third column you have the object of the verb. with each noun in the subject or object, there is (in brackets) is pronoun.

Subject Pronoun Verb Pronoun Object
The Teacher (I) Sees (See) The Boy (Him)
The Boy (He) Sees The Teacher (Me)
The Girl (She) Sees The Teacher (Me)
The Teacher (I) Sees (See) The Girl (her)
The Boys (They) See The Teacher (us)
The Teachers (We) See The Boys (Them)

The form of the pronoun in the subject column is nominative.
The form of the pronoun in The object column is objective.
After a preposition, the pronoun is always in the objective form.

Here is the list of the two forms of the pronouns:

Nominative Object Nominative Object
I me It it
you you we us
he him they them
she her


    Pronouns are in the Nominative Case when they are the subject of the verb.
They are in the objective case:
(I) they are object the verb.
(II) after the preposition.

Nominative Verb Objective Preposition Objective
I know him and I Write to him
You know me and You Write to me
He knows her and He Write to her
She knows us and She Write to us
We know them and We Write to them
They know you and they Write to you

Objects

    One or two verbs like tell and give, often have two objects.
one of them is generally a person, the other a thing, e.g

Subject (Nominative) Verb Object1 Object2
I am giving the students a lesson
He can tell you the answer

The story without words.


     Now after that rather difficult grammar, here is something different, somthing pleasant. it is a story in a pictures about a poor man, Mr. needy, and a rich man Mr. wealthy. Mr. wealthy has a lot of money, a big house, and manservant, Mr. needy has not much money, but he has a cornet in the street outside.

Look at the picture on the right of this page then answer these question about them. Use these words in your answers:

play, cornet, ear, pound-note.

where is he playing his cornet?
👉 Picture (2). does Mr. wealthy like the noise?
where is he putting his fingers? why is he doing this?

👉 Picture (4). who are the people in this picture? what is Mr. wealthy holding in his hand? which hand is he holding the pound-notes? how many pound-note are there in his hand? what is he saying to the manservant?
👉 Picture (5). who has the pound-notes now? which hand is he holding them in? does he look happy? what is the manservant doing with the cornet? in which hand is he carrying the cornet?

👉 Picture (7). where is the cornet now?
