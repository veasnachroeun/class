Seventeenth Lesson)

The characters in the essential english Books (2)


Mrs. Priestley AND Some Other

     You already know Mr. Priestley, the teacher and writer. In this lesson you will hear about his wife, Mrs. Priestley, the Priestley's house, and some of the people in the house. Mrs. Priestley knows all about the house, She does the work in it every day​​, and to-day she will tell you a little about it. Mrs. Priestley is a pleasant-looking woman of about forty, with warm brown hair and soft dark brown eyes. she is kind and gentle, but she manage her house (and, in her quiet way, her husband) very well. he is of course, a clever man but a little unpractical, and he need Mrs Priestley to look after him. Mrs Priestley on the other hand, is very practical and full of common sense. the priestleys have two children Jhon and Magaret. Jhon is eighteen, six foot tall, and a fine mainly fellow. he is at the university and is studying to be a doctor. he is a clever, hard-working student a first-class footballer, boxer, and runner. He is strong both in body and character, and quiet and thoughtful like his father. He will make a good doctor.
      Margaret is on eleven. She is a lovely little girl with golden hair and dark blue eyes and a spirit that is always bright and happy full of joy and gaiety. She is not fond of study of any kind, but she loves music and dancing and she sings very prettily. she is like a ray of sunshine in the house. Mr Priestley is very fond of his son Jhon and proud of him; and Magaret is the apple of his eye.
    But here comes Mrs. Priestley. "good morning, mrs. Priestley; how are you"?
Mrs. Priestley: Good morning, everyone. I am very well, thank you. You want to know about my house. Well, I am very please to be here and I will gladly tell you something about it.
The house is rather big, and there is a lot of work in it, so I can not do everything alone. I have Susan and Lizzie to help me with the work. Lizzie is our cook, and she is very good cook, but she is no good at all at housework. Now Susan is very good at housework, but she can not cook at all, she can't even boil an egg.
    But that is all right, for lazzie gets on with her cooking, and Susan and I do the housework and believe me we all work very hard Susan and lazzie get up at quarter to seven and make the fire and open the windows. I get up at half past seven and so does my husband, it time for breakfast at eight o'clock.
     We have breakfast in the breakfast-room. I like to begin the day well, so our breakfast is always a good one.
    My husband reads his newspaper and smoke a cigarette with his last cup of tea, before he begin his work at nine o'clock.
    The lizzie and susan begin to clean the house, wash up, make the beds and get vegetables ready for lunch. We have lunch at one o'clock after that I do some sewing, or go out for a walk, or go to see people.
    We have afternoon tea about five o'clock, but for that we do not go into the dinning_room. We go to sitting-room, and Susan bring in the tea with plates of bread and butter and small cakes.
    After tea we sit and talk or listen to the wireless or read. Sometime we go to the cinema or the theatre. About seven or eight o'clock we have dinner or supper, and at eleven o'clock I generally go to bed, but my husband likes to sit up late and read or write in his study.
    Well, that is all for the present. I will tell you more about the house some other time.
